Pandemic Response Performance Comparison

Associate with COVID-19 Statistics Analysis

Introduction

The 2019 COVID-19 pandemic is the most concerned thing in the world now and will be an important event in human history. As most people in the world are now acutely aware, an outbreak of COVID-19 was detected in December of 2019. As of this writing, every continent in the world has been affected by this highly contagious disease. From the statistics of Johns Hopkins university, there are total 69 million confirmed cases in the world. And over 1.58 million deaths over the world.

About Data

The COVID-19 data is mainly from Johns Hopkins University. And the Education data is from United States Department of Agriculture. In addition, the age data for states is from KAISER FAMILY FOUNDATION.

Objectives

The primary objective of this project is to visualize the cases statistics of coronavirus for different countries. The cases statistics includes the confirmed cases, test cases and death cases. To explore the factors affected the confirmed cases, the average education level, economics statistics will be compared with the cases data. And the cases statistics will be analyzed verse healthcare spending and GDP for each country to help measuring the response performance in the pandemic for different countries. The cases data comes from Johns Hopkins Coronavirus Resource Center and the education & economics data will come from United States Department of Agriculture.
